Before improving a process, question whether the requirement still matters. Each requirement should have a responsible owner rather than being protected by old habit or departmental default. If a step should not exist, optimizing it, scripting it, or attaching automation only makes the wrong process more stable.

Deletion and simplification are not cleanup after optimization. They are the entry point before speed, parallel work, automation, or tooling. Only a step that remains necessary should move into acceleration.
